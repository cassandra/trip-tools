Reviews

Capture your thoughts, share your experiences.

The Problems

  • Different review sites for different things: Google for restaurants, Booking.com for hotels, TripAdvisor for tours.
  • The same experience might warrant reviews on multiple platforms.
  • No place to capture raw thoughts right after an experience, before you forget the details.
  • Polishing a quick note into a proper review happens later - but where did you put that note?
  • When writing your travel journal, you want to reference your reviews but can't remember where you posted them.
  • Reviews you write are scattered and disconnected from your trip record.

The Solution

A central place to capture, refine, and track all your trip reviews:

  • Capture in the moment
    • Quick notes right after an experience while details are fresh.
    • Link reviews to locations from your trip.
    • Rate and categorize (restaurant, hotel, activity, etc.).
  • Refine at your pace
    • Draft and polished versions of each review.
    • Edit and improve when you have time.
    • No pressure to post immediately.
  • Post where it matters
    • Copy to Google, TripAdvisor, Booking.com, or wherever is appropriate.
    • Track where you've posted each review.
    • Future: direct posting integration.
  • Connected to your journal
    • Reference reviews while writing journal entries.
    • Pull quotes and details into your travelog.
    • Complete record of your trip experiences.

Existing Tools

  • Google Maps
    • Convenient for restaurant reviews - visible where people search.
    • Reviews live on Google, not connected to your trip.
    • No draft workflow; post immediately or lose your thoughts.
  • Booking.com
    • Verified stay reviews carry credibility.
    • Only for accommodations booked through them.
    • Reviews belong to Booking.com, not your trip record.
  • TripAdvisor
    • Broad coverage of tours, activities, and attractions.
    • Increasingly cluttered with ads and sponsored content.
    • No connection to your other trip data.
  • Notes apps
    • Flexible for capturing thoughts.
    • No structure, categorization, or trip context.
    • Easy to forget where you put things.
