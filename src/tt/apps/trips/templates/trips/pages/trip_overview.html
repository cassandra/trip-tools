Trip Overview

Your trip at a glance.

The Problems

  • Trip information is scattered across multiple apps, tabs, and documents.
  • What you need to see depends on where you are in the trip lifecycle.
  • No single place shows you the most important information for right now.
  • Switching between planning, booking, traveling, and post-trip modes requires mental overhead.

The Solution

A contextual dashboard that adapts to your trip's current phase, surfacing what matters most:

  • Planning phase
    • Quick access to locations under consideration.
    • Status of your planning decisions and comparisons.
    • Link to Planning for detailed candidate management.
  • Booking phase
    • Itinerary status at a glance.
    • What's booked vs. what still needs booking.
    • Upcoming booking deadlines and reminders.
    • Link to Bookings for detailed management.
  • During the trip
    • Today's itinerary and upcoming activities.
    • Map of relevant locations.
    • Quick access to add journal notes and reviews.
    • Link to Itinerary for the full schedule.
  • Post-trip
    • Journal writing status and prompts.
    • Image upload and organization.
    • Publishing status for your travelog.
    • Link to Journal for full editing.

Existing Tools

  • TripIt
    • Good at aggregating bookings into an itinerary view.
    • Doesn't adapt to trip phase or support pre-booking planning.
    • No post-trip journaling or sharing features.
  • Google Trips (discontinued)
    • Had potential as a unified trip view.
    • Was discontinued, leaving a gap in the market.
  • Notion / Spreadsheets
    • Flexible but require manual setup and maintenance.
    • No trip-aware context or phase adaptation.
