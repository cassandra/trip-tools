The Problems

  • Building an itinerary means manually copying information from bookings, research, and notes.
  • When plans change, you update the itinerary separately from the booking - easy to get out of sync.
  • Not everything in your day is a booking (walking tours, free attractions, meals), but it still needs to be in the itinerary.
  • Hard to see if your day is realistic: travel times, opening hours, and logistics aren't connected.
  • Sharing the itinerary with travel companions means exporting or screenshot workflows.

The Solution

An itinerary deeply integrated with your trip data that stays current automatically:

  • Connected to locations
    • Add locations directly to your itinerary.
    • See addresses, hours, and notes without switching contexts.
    • Map view shows each day's geography.
  • Linked to bookings
    • Bookings automatically appear in your itinerary.
    • Changes to bookings update the itinerary.
    • Clear distinction between booked and unbooked activities.
  • Flexible daily planning
    • Mix bookings with free activities, meals, and downtime.
    • Reorder and adjust as plans evolve.
    • Notes and reminders for each day.
  • Shared with your group
    • All trip members see the same itinerary.
    • Changes sync automatically.
    • No export/import workflows needed.

Existing Tools

  • TripIt
    • Good at parsing confirmation emails into itinerary items.
    • Assumes everything is a booking - awkward for free activities.
    • User interface feels dated and clunky.
    • Limited development since acquisition; API discontinued.
  • Wanderlog
    • Modern interface with good map integration.
    • Itinerary building is manual; limited booking integration.
    • Free tier is restrictive; pushes toward paid plans.
  • Spreadsheets
    • Completely flexible but entirely manual.
    • No connection to locations, maps, or bookings.
    • Collaboration works but lacks trip-specific features.
