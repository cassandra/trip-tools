The Problems

  • Information about places is scattered across bookmarks, notes, messages, and your memory.
  • No central place to track what you know about a location: who recommended it, ratings, hours, pricing, booking status.
  • Hard to see all your places on a map to understand geography and plan logistics.
  • Location data doesn't flow into other trip activities (itinerary, bookings, journal).
  • When someone asks "where should we eat near the museum?" you're back to searching from scratch.

The Solution

Location as the central organizing concept that ties all trip data together:

  • Rich location profiles
    • Store recommendations, sources, and why it's on your list.
    • Track ratings, hours, pricing, and contact info.
    • Note booking requirements and status.
    • Link to your reviews after visiting.
  • Map integration
    • See all locations plotted geographically.
    • Understand distances and clusters for daily planning.
    • Use during the trip for navigation context.
  • Connected to everything
    • Locations feed into transportation and lodging decisions.
    • Locations anchor your daily itinerary.
    • Locations appear in your journal entries.

Existing Tools

  • Google Maps
    • Great for visual plotting and navigation.
    • Saved places and lists are too basic for trip planning.
    • "My Maps" is more capable but has a clunky interface and limited attributes.
    • Doesn't integrate with itinerary, bookings, or journaling.
  • Wanderlog / Tripit
    • Include location features tied to itinerary.
    • Limited flexibility for pre-trip research and comparison.
    • Locations are secondary to the itinerary, not first-class objects.
  • Notes / Spreadsheets
    • Flexible but no map visualization.
    • Manual effort to maintain and cross-reference.
    • Disconnected from other trip tools.
