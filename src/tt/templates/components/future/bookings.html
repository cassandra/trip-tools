The Problems

  • Confirmations are scattered across email, apps, and PDFs - no single view of what's booked.
  • No clear connection between "things to book" and "things already booked."
  • Booking to-dos require separate task lists and calendar reminders.
  • Bookings and itinerary are related but managed separately - easy to lose sync.
  • Tracking who paid for what and splitting costs requires yet another app.
  • Cancellation policies and deadlines are buried in confirmation emails.

The Solution

Bookings as the financial and operational layer of your trip, tightly connected to your itinerary:

  • Unified booking view
    • All bookings in one place: flights, hotels, activities, restaurants.
    • Link to confirmation details and reference numbers.
    • Track booking status: confirmed, pending, cancelled.
  • To-book task management
    • Track what still needs to be booked.
    • Set deadlines and reminders for time-sensitive bookings.
    • Convert to-book items into confirmed bookings.
  • Financial tracking
    • Record costs, payment methods, and charge dates.
    • Track cancellation policies and refund deadlines.
    • Split expenses among trip members.
  • Itinerary integration
    • Bookings automatically appear in your itinerary.
    • Changes stay synchronized.
    • Clear view of what's booked vs. planned but not yet booked.

Existing Tools

  • TripIt
    • Good at parsing confirmation emails into booking records.
    • No concept of "to-book" items - only tracks what's already booked.
    • Limited financial tracking; no expense splitting.
    • API discontinued; limited integration options.
  • Tricount / Splitwise
    • Excellent for splitting expenses among a group.
    • Completely disconnected from trip planning and itinerary.
    • Manual entry of every expense.
  • Email + Spreadsheets
    • Search email for confirmations; track costs in a spreadsheet.
    • Entirely manual and error-prone.
    • No reminders, no status tracking, no integration.
